Endangered Species

The Tonkin Snub Nosed Monkey, scientifically known as Rhinopithecus avunculus, is one of the most endangered primate species in the world. With an estimated population of less than 300 individuals, it is critically endangered and faces numerous threats to its survival.

The Tonkin Snub Nosed Monkey is characterized by its distinctive physical features. It has a short and upturned nose, hence the name “snub-nosed.” Additionally, its fur is predominantly black, with white markings on its face, lips, and legs, giving it a striking and memorable appearance.

These monkeys are endemic to the remote mountainous regions of northern Vietnam, specifically in the provinces of Thanh Hoa, Nghe An, and Ha Tinh. They are perfectly adapted to living in high-altitude forests, often found at elevations between 1,200 and 2,200 meters.
