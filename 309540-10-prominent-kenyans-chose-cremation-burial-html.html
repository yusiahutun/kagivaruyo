10 prominent Kenyans who chose cremation over burial

The late Safaricom CEO Robert (Bob) William Collymore on Tuesday, July 2, joined a growing list of high-profile Kenyan personalities who were cremated in compliance with their wishes.

Collymore's body was burnt to ashes at Kariokor crematorium in a private function restricted only to Safaricom's top officials, family members and a few state officers.

Although cremation is not a popular method of disposing human corpses in the country and is even frowned at by a section of Kenyans, a good number of prominent Kenyans chose cremation instead of the grave.

Below are the 10 Kenyan celebrities who settled for cremation instead of burial, starting with Collymore:

1. Robert (Bob) William Collymore

The Safaricom CEO became the latest high-profile Kenyan personality to be cremated. As already indicated, Collymore's body was disposed at Kariokor crematorium in Nairobi on July 2, 2019.

2. Wangari Maathai

The renowned Kenyan environmental and political activist who was also the first African woman to win the Nobel Prize died in September 2011 and was cremated at Kariokor crematorium.

3. Kenneth Matiba

Matiba was a Kenyan politician and an activist for democracy. He vied for the presidency in 1992 and came second and tried again in 2007 but was unsuccessful.

He passed on in April 2018 and was cremated at Langata Cemetery, Nairobi.

4. Jeremiah Gitau Kiereini

The late tycoon and former head of public service passed on in May 2019 at the age of 90 and was cremated in a highly private function.

5. Peter Habenga Okondo

The late former labour minister who served in retired President Daniel Arap Moi's government was controversially cremated in 1996,.

The exercise reportedly caused major rift between his family and his Banyala clan.

6. Jane Kiano

The former chairperson of the Maendeleo ya Wanawake Organisation and widow of late former Cabinet minister Julius Gikonyo Kiano died of lung cancer at Nairobi Hospital.

She was cremated in October 2018 at Kariokor crematorium.

7. Kenneth Binyavanga Wainaina

Binyavanga was a Kenyan gay activist, author, journalist and 2002 winner of the Caine Prize for African Writing. He died in May 2019 and was also cremated.

8. John Macharia

Macharia, a businessman and son of Royal Media Services (RMS) founder SK Macharia, died in a road accident in Nairobi and was cremated in April 2018.

9. Manasses Kuria and wife Mary Kuria

Manasses Kuria was a Kenyan Anglican Archbishop and the second African Archbishop and Bishop of Nairobi in the Anglican Church of Kenya. He was cremated in September 2005.

His wife Mary died in July 2002 at the age of 73 and was also cremated despite the heated debate and division that cremation of her bishop husband sparked in the church.

10. Joshua Okuthe and wife Ruth Florence Okuthe

The former sports administrator was cremated in July 2009 at Kariokor Cemetery. The same was the case with his first wife Florence.

Although the scripture does not forbid cremation, burial remains the norm and is preferred by most Christians in Kenya.

Many people find closure in burial than cremation, which is a common practice among Hindus and is more of a religious ritual.

Cremated remains are commonly referred to as ashes, but in reality they consist primarily of bone fragments.

Once the cremation is done, the ashes are usually transferred to either a temporary container or an urn provided by the family of the deceased.

In Nairobi, the cost of cremation reportedly ranges between KSh 13,000 for adults and KSh 6,000 for children .
