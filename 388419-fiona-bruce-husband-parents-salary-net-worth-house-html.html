Fiona Bruce husband, parents, salary, net worth, house

Fiona Bruce is a Singapore-born British journalist, newsreader and television presenter who has worked for the BBC for about four decades. She is a household name thanks to her role in presenting several flagship programmes at BBC including Fake or Fortune, Crimewatch and Antiques Roadshow. She is also known for being an excellent conference facilitator and after dinner speaker who is fluent in a handful of languages and topics.

The life of Fiona Bruce is not different from that of any other journalist. Once in a while, she cannot help but find herself in the middle of a controversy. When she hosted MP Diane Abbott in Question Time, it resulted in her being caught up in a row with the Labour Party.

She was accused of repeating Tory propaganda. Such criticism and accusations come with the job, but with her experience, she can handle anything thrown at her.

Fiona Bruce biography

Fiona Bruce was born on the 25th of April, 1964 in Singapore to an English mother (Rosemary Bruce) and a Scottish father (John Bruce). Her father was a postboy but worked his way up to be a regional managing director of Unilever. Fiona Bruce parents brought up a family of three children.

The journalist attended several schools including, the Gayton Primary School in Wirral, the International School of Milan and the Haberdashers' Aske’s Hatcham College in New Cross, London. She also studied French and Italian at Hertford College, Oxford.

How many languages does Fiona Bruce speak? She speaks three languages that include English, Italian and French.

Career

A management consulting firm hired Bruce upon completing her education. She worked there for one year but found the experience short of her expectations as it was depressingly dull. When she left the management consulting firm, she landed jobs at several advertising agencies.

This includes Boase Massimi Pollitt, where she met her future husband, who was the company's director at the time. Bruce attended a wedding where she met Tim Gardam. He was the editor of BBC’s Panorama. In 1989, he hired her as a researcher on the programme.

Within a short time, she rose to become an assistant producer of the programme. In 1992, she made the change to reporting on Breakfast News. This saw her move to BBC South East where she appeared as an occasional presenter and reporter on Newsroom South East and First Sight, the current affairs programme that aired weekly.

In 1999, she became the secondary presenter of the BBC Six O’Clock News. She presented as cover for the main presenter Huw Edwards and regularly on Fridays until 2003.

Bruce and Edwards moved to the BBC Ten O’Clock News as co-presenters making Bruce the first female presenter of the bulletin. After four years, she went back to presenting the BBC News at Six and remained there for eleven years until January 2019 when she stepped down. Other programmes she has presented include:
