How to apply for a new number plate in Kenya: a step-by-step guide

You cannot drive any motorized vehicle on a Kenyan road if it has not been fitted with number plates. If you have recently lost or damaged your plates, there is no need to panic because they can be replaced. All you need to do is follow the laid down procedure on how to apply for a new number plate in Kenya, and you are good to go.

Vehicle registration plates are usually made from metal or plastic and attached to a motor vehicle or trailer for identification purposes. Throughout the centuries of automobile use, number plates have become an essential tool for protecting drivers. They allow for the identification of vehicles caught in accidents and effective enforcement of the law on the road.

How to apply for a new number plate in Kenya

The Registrar of Motor vehicles handles the registration and licensing of motor vehicles. So what documents do you need for new number plates? If you have imported a new car, you will require the following documents when applying for a new number plate:

  • Copy of national ID of the vehicle owner or a valid Passport.
  • KRA Pin certificate
  • Import entry form (Form 63)
  • Airway Bill
  • Insurance cover
  • Duty and VAT receipts
  • Import Declaration Form (IDF)
  • Foreign log book translated into English
  • Port Release Order
  • Vehicle Inspection Report (VIR) if the vehicle is for commercial use.
  • Foreign Vehicle Receipt/License
  • Clean Report of Findings (CRF)
  • A temporary Importation Document (Form C44A or C44)
  • A duly filled Form A personally signed by the importer.
  • Authority to enter goods for home use from the Commissioner of Customs & Excise
  • Road manifest or Carnet de Passages in the case of vehicles imported by road

Once you have confirmed that you have all the required documents, you can proceed to apply for the new number plate. If the registrar accepts your request, the vehicle will be registered, and you will be issued with new number plates.

NTSA KG plates

KG (Kenya Garage) plates are short-term dealership/garage plates with a special insurance cover for vehicles awaiting new number plates or without insurance. NTSA issues these plates to individuals who wish to move newly imported vehicles from one town to another as they wait to be issued with official number plates.

How to apply for a lost number plate in Kenya

Driving without a number plate affixed to your car violates Section 12(1) of the Traffic Act, which provides that no motor vehicle shall be used on a road unless the identification plates are fixed in the prescribed manner.

Therefore, if you have lost your number plate, you need to replace it immediately. Here is a simple procedure for the replacement of a number plate you should follow.

  • Begin by visiting a police station near you to report the loss or damage. The police officer will issue an abstract.
  • Visit the nearest DCI offices with the original logbook and police abstract to obtain a tape lifting report verifying the vehicle engine and chassis number.
  • An affidavit from the commissioner of oaths is also required for a lost or damaged number plate.
  • All these documents should be scanned and saved on the computer.
  • Next, log in to your NTSA TIMS account.
  • Once you are logged in, click on the Vehicle Registration tab and choose Apply for Reflective Number plate from the list of options available.
  • Select the car whose number plate is lost or defaced from the list of vehicles that appears and click on apply.
  • Select either lost or defaced.
  • Choose the type of plate you are applying for, either square or oblong.
  • Fill in all the required details and provide the ID number of the person who will collect the plates.
  • Upload the required documents and choose the station where you wish to pick the plates from.
  • Pay the required fee to complete the application process.
  • You will be notified via SMS when your number plate is ready for collection.

When collecting the number plates, ensure that you carry the old ones with you in the case of damaged plates. The person authorized to collect the number plates should carry their original national ID.

In case of companies, a letter of authority to collect the plates is required. Before receiving the replacement plates, you are advised to print out the number plate and fix it on the screen.

Customized number plate in Kenya

Custom number plates adorn the look of your vehicle and at the same time speak a lot about you, your choices and your personality. If you're a civilian and want to stand out from the rest on the roads, you can have a customized number plate. These number plates are issued by NTSA.

To get one, you will follow the above procedure on how to apply for a new number plate and submit the required documents. The uptake of these special plates is low in Kenya, given the high cost associated with this mark of distinction.

It is imperative to note that the installation of an illegal customized number plate on your car attracts a fine of Kshs. 300,000 or a 12-month jail sentence.

How much does a number plate cost in Kenya?

The cost varies depending on the type of number plate you choose. Having your name inscribed on the metal plate will cost you Kshs. 1 million. If you want to get a plate that matches your lucky triple numbers, you will be required to pay Kshs. 30,000. The annual charge of maintaining the customized plates amounts to Kshs. 50,000.

The replacement costs also vary depending on the type of plate. Replacement of a lost or damaged customized number plate costs Kshs. 50,000. On the other hand, replacing a single ordinary number plate, either square or oblong, will cost you Kshs. 1050. If you wish to replace both plates, you will be required to pay Kshs. 2050.

The above step by step guide on how to apply for a new number plate in Kenya should come in handy if you have lost or damaged yours. If you have lost your plate, you should begin the replacement process immediately to avoid rubbing shoulders with the law enforcement authorities.

Since 2018, buyers of new cars are obliged to secure an NTSA Third Licence Inspection Sticker. Car owners in Kenya are required to pay Ksh. 700 for a third licence plate. The warrant is valid for 10 years and is renewable at the exact cost of acquiring it. In case of damage or an accident before the duration lapses, motorists are obliged to pay Ksh. 700 for a replacement.
