65 best questions to ask guest speakers and professionals

You might have been invited to attend an event featuring professionals as they share their insights on a given topic. If the mike rolls in your direction, you need to have a list of questions to ask a guest speaker so that you can make the most out of the golden opportunity. These questions should be based on the content of the speech and those of interest to the speaker.

Asking questions is a normal part of any extended conversation in your personal and professional life. It is the pathway to real information on a given topic. Whether you are looking for solutions to problems, establishing a better working relationship, shaping your vision, or negotiating, asking the right questions is a vital technique.

65 best questions to ask a guest speaker

Asking questions to a guest speaker is important because it helps to create an interactive dialogue between the speaker and the audience. Questions provide an opportunity to gain insight into the speaker's opinion or expertise on a certain topic, as well as to learn more about the speaker's professional journey. Below is a list of questions to ask guest speakers during a virtual event or any normal meeting.

What are good questions to ask a presenter after a presentation?

Finding the right questions to ask a guest speaker is no easy task. You do not want to find yourself in a situation where you ask a dumb question, and everyone in the room gives you a scornful look. If you are stuck on what to say, you can check out this list of questions to ask a great speaker.

  • How did your earlier career choices lead you to where you are now?
  • What is the biggest risk you have taken?
  • What is the worst job decision you ever made?
  • What drove you into this field and motivated you to stick along?
  • How has the industry changed in the last five years? What do you predict will happen in the next five years?
  • What have you lost on the way to achieving what you have today?
  • How long have you worked in this field?
  • What has helped you get to where you are, and what advice would you give someone who wants to set off in a similar direction?
  • Which has been more valuable in your career, education or experience?
  • What is the best job decision you ever made?
  • What is one piece of practical advice you would give to someone new in this field?
  • How did you set yourself apart from the candidates who wanted the same job?
  • Was there a career setback you faced that you later realized was an advantage?
  • How do you separate your feelings and emotions from difficult decisions?
  • What would you do differently if you could go back in time?

  • What are people's common misconceptions, and how can we combat them?

Questions about public speaking

A public speech is when you give a long talk to a group of people to inform or entertain. Public speaking is one of the greatest fears of an average human. Just the thought of speaking on the stage can make any person a nervous wreck. Here are some questions you can ask to improve your speaking skills to secure your future in the industry.

  • What motivates you to do what you do?
  • Where does your passion come from?
  • What are you most afraid of?
  • What do you want the audience to do differently after listening to your presentation?
  • What one word best sums up you and your vision?
  • What do you want the audience to remember from your presentation?
  • What made you decide to tackle this subject?
  • What do you love most about the art of public speaking?
  • When did you realize you wanted to dedicate your life to this noble cause?
  • In what ways does the relationship between the speaker and the audience sustain you?
  • What experience from the past haunts you about public speaking?
  • Who are the speakers that you envy?
  • What are your best memories of introducing a speaker?
  • What do you want the audience to be saying about you afterwards?

Questions to ask a potential keynote speaker

The role of the keynote speaker is to make speeches that often will define the event's theme. If you have shortlisted several keynote speakers for your event, you will face a daunting task when choosing the right candidate. Here is a set of questions you can ask the potential speakers before booking them.

  • How will you align your speech with the company's vision and strategy?
  • How is this speech different from your last?
  • Do you like to interact with the audience? If so, how so?
  • How long is the perfect speech?
  • Do you have content that will engage the audience before and after the event?
  • What are your speech objectives and learning points?
  • How will you ensure that your presentation is memorable to all attendees?
  • Have you worked in the industry before?
  • Do the topics, speaking style, and content match the audience's needs?
  • What preparations will you make to ensure a successful presentation?
  • Who are some other keynote speakers whose work you admire?

How do you interview a guest speaker?

Interviewing a guest speaker can be a great way to get their insights and opinions on a subject. To ensure you get the most out of your interview, it's important to prepare ahead of time. Start by understanding your guest's background and the topic they will be discussing so you can craft relevant questions.

During the interview, make sure to listen carefully to their responses and follow up with any clarifying questions. It's also important to be respectful and to make sure you provide them with enough time to answer your questions.

What 4 things should every speaker consider?

Every speaker should consider the following four things:

  • Their audience and what they are looking to gain from the presentation.
  • The message they want to deliver during the presentation.
  • The structure of the presentation, including how to best organize the content.
  • How to engage and interact with the audience to ensure they stay interested and receptive throughout the presentation.

What is the golden rules of public speaking?

Some of the golden rules of public speaking are:

  • Be prepared: Research your topic and know your material.
  • Speak with confidence: Believe in yourself and your message.
  • Make eye contact: Engage your audience by making eye contact with individuals in the room.
  • Use gestures: Incorporate natural hand gestures to emphasize your points.
