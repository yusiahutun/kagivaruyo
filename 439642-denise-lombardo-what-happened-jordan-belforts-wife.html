Education

She also enrolled for a Business Administration and Science course at Towson University. Denise was determined to study up to the master's level, and she enrolled at the Australian Catholic University. She graduated with a master's in Educational Leadership and Administration General.

Unfortunately, Belfort's employment at L.F. Rothschild was short-lived, as the company terminated him in 1987 due to financial challenges arising from the Black Monday stock market crash.

Belfort went on to establish the Stratton Oakmont franchise, specializing in the promotion of penny stocks and engaging in deceptive "pump & dump" stock sales that defrauded investors.

At its peak, Stratton Oakmont employed over 1,000 stock brokers and conducted transactions involving stocks valued at over $1 billion. However, Belfort's extravagant lifestyle and recreational narcotics addiction became notorious during his tenure at Stratton Oakmont.

The National Association of Securities Dealers (NASD) initiated an investigation into the company in 1989. While all this was going on, his marriage was taking another turn, and unfortunately, they parted ways in 1991 after staying together for four years.

In December 1996, the NASD shuttered Stratton Oakmont, leading to Jordan Belfort's arrest in 1999 on charges of securities fraud and money laundering.
