20+ best Nigerian movies that you should watch in 2024

Nigerian movies have been part of African culture for decades now. The country's movie industry has seen tremendous growth and upgrades in the past few years. This has directly translated into better movies, with many films recognized for their world-class production. If you want something new to watch, look for the best Nigerian movies worth watching in 2024. You will be glad you chose the genre.

This list of the best Nigerian movies are those that have a well-crafted storyline and dynamic casts. You can watch these movies on Netflix or YouTube. They are ranked according to their IMDb rating.

1. Omo Ghetto: The Saga

Omo Ghetto: The Saga, also known as Omo Ghetto 2, is a Nigerian gangster comedy film that has had fans hooked for years. It is the second film in the Omo Ghetto franchise and is the sequel to the 2010 trilogy film Omo Ghetto.

The story follows reunited twins: one good-hearted female gangster and her uptight rich sister as they take on family, crime, cops, and all the trouble that follows them. The fun movie has an excellent cast and some celebrity cameos you do not want to miss.

2. Castle & Castle

Castle & Castle is Nigeria’s and Africa’s first legal series that you should watch. The show follows a pair of high-powered, successful lawyers who find themselves defending opposite interests of the justice systems, which puts a strain on their marriage. Castle & Castle is about love and betrayal and is one of the best Nollywood movies that should be on your list.

3. Shadow Parties

Subscribe to watch new videos

Shadow Parties is a Nollywood political drama film directed and produced by Yemi Amodu. The film centres around a long-standing conflict between two neighbouring communities, Aje and Iludun. This conflict has tragically claimed lives, including the parents of Arike, who is married to Aremu from Aje.

The film was released in 2020 and featured Yemi Blaq, Omotola Jalade-Ekeinde, Toyin Abraham, Jide Kosoko, Sola Sobowale, Magdalena Korpas, and Lucien Morgan. The film explores the devastating impact of this cycle of violence on families caught in the middle.

7. Taxi Driver: Oko Ashewo

Taxi Driver: Oko Ashewo follows the story of Adigun, a naive mechanic from the village, who comes to Lagos for the first time after his estranged father's death. He gets a job driving an older man's taxi. He meets many weird people during work, such as prostitutes, assassins, and a weird gang.

The film explores the contrast between Adigun's rural background and the fast-paced, unconventional world of Lagos at night.

8. Citation

Subscribe to watch new videos

Citation is a 2020 film that follows a university student who speaks up after a professor attempts to assault her. Based on real events, the story catalogues the university’s reaction to her claims. Citation centres on Moremi, a smart and determined college student who accuses a celebrated professor of assaulting her.

The ensuing drama unfolds over a tribunal where the university’s authorities listen to her testimony. Moremi’s allegations aren’t believed from the start, and viewers are shown flashbacks which reveal the reality. Shortly after its release, Citation rose to the sixth-most popular film on Netflix and was the most-watched on Netflix in Nigeria.

10. The Blood Covenant

The Blood Covent is one of the newest Nigerian movies released in 2022. The story centres around three ambitious young men in their late twenties: Eddy, Jite, and Osiano. Struggling to make ends meet and tired of being looked down upon, they yearn for a life of luxury.

This desperation leads them down a dark path as they become involved by the allure of rituals and blood money. The film was directed by Fiyin Gambo and stars Uzor Arukwe, Shalewa Ashafa, Oluwatobi Bakre and Demi Banwo.

11. Blood Sisters

Subscribe to watch new videos

Blood Sisters is a Nigerian thriller series that premiered in 2022 and streamed exclusively on Netflix. The series, which is the first Nigerian Netflix Original, features an ensemble cast that includes Ramsey Noah, Nancy Isime, Kate Henshaw, Wale Ojo, Ini Dima-Okojie and many other notable talents.

The story revolves around two best friends, Sarah and Kemi, who are bound by a dangerous secret. The two are forced to go on the run after a wealthy groom disappears during his engagement party.

When the Blood Sisters starts, viewers meet the two ladies digging in the ground and suddenly pushing a wrapped-up body into the hole. Blood Sisters continues with great storytelling until the last scene.

12. Black November

Black November is a 2012 Nigerian-American action drama film directed by Jeta Amata. The film tackles the real-life struggles of the Niger Delta, a region rich in oil but plagued by environmental damage and poverty. The story follows a community fighting back against the destruction of their land caused by excessive drilling and oil spills by a multinational oil corporation.

The Black Book is one of the latest Nollywood films released in 2023. It tells the story of a man named Paul Edima who confronts his dark past to avenge his son. His world shatters when his son, Damilola, is framed for a kidnapping and killed.

To clear his son's name and get justice, Paul abandons his peaceful life and unleashes his deadly skills. His target is a powerful and corrupt police gang led by his former boss, General Issa.

14. Namaste Wahala

Namaste Wahala, which loosely translates to Hello Trouble, is a Nigerian cross-cultural romantic comedy film. The film starring Ini Dima-Okojie and Ruslaan Mumtaz is the first major collaboration between Bollywood and Nollywood.

The film is based on a romantic relationship between an Indian man and a Nigerian woman despite coming from two completely different backgrounds. The interracial romance hits humorous and heart-wrenching hiccups as the couple’s parents fight against the relationship.

Christmas in Miami is a 2021 Nigerian comedy film that follows the adventures of Akpos, a character known for getting into trouble. Akpos travels with his family to Miami to compete in a festive competition called the Intercontinental Christmas Fiesta.
