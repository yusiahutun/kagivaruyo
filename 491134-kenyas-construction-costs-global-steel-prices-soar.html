Kenya's Construction Costs Up as Global Steel Prices Soar

  • Steel prices increased globally in three months following a market shortage of the steel supply
  • Leading global market suppliers like China (52%) reported a slump in crude steel production, resulting in high prices
  • Market indicators showed a 29.5% rise in steel prices from $1458.37 (KSh 181,057) per tonne reported in October 2022 to $1964.12 (KSh 243,845) in January 2023
  • The rise in prices leading to increased cost of construction could be transferred to the end users

The cost of construction could spike as steel prices in the global market hit a new high in three months.

Global market reports showed a tonne of steel sales at $1964.12 (KSh 243,845) in January 2023.

Steel prices

The prices increased by 29.5% up from $1458.37 (KSh 181,057) per tonne of steel reported in October 2023.

The increase in prices is attributed to a drop in crude steel production which led to a supply shortage.

Kenya's top import market source China, which produces 50% of the global supply, reported a slump in steel production.

According to the Architectural Association of Kenya (AAK) official Patience Mulondo, the cost is likely to be transferred to end users.

“The increase in the cost of construction, which is transferred to the end user, could also affect key projects like affordable housing and infrastructure development,” said Mulando, as quoted by Business Daily.

Mulando urged the government to look into the trends and cushion Kenyans from the rising prices.

Steel prices in Kenya

As of October 2022, steel prices in Kenya ranged from KSh 500 to KSh 4,000 depending on the brand and quality of the metal.

According to CK, steel prices range based on Diameter in October included; D8 – KSh 520, D10 – KSh 820, D12 – KSh 1,220, D16 – KSh 2,230, D20 – KSh 3,500, D25 – KSh 5,700, D32 – KSh 9,800 and Binding Wire KSh 3,700, Hoop Iron KSh 3,000 (per roll).
