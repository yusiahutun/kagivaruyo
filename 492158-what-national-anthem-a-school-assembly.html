What to say before the national anthem in a school assembly

At school, you learn so much. During your formative years, you learnt how to read and write, and now you can form logical sentences and develop new ideas. Of course, it doesn't end there; learning institutions also teach students and pupils public speaking, one of the most important in adult life. And one of the best ways of doing this is during the morning assembly. So, what should you say if it is your turn to move the crowd with inspirational words? It would be great if you learnt what to say before the national anthem in a school assembly to avoid embarrassing yourself. And there are so many ways you can get it right.

A school assembly is a simple gathering of your peers, or other students, with whom you normally interact in class or the hallways. However, you might be dumbstruck when offered a chance to speak to the familiar crowd during a morning assembly. That is why you should come prepared with a national anthem anchoring script. Something that will wow the crowd. So, what should you state before starting the national anthem during a morning assembly?

What to say before the national anthem in a school assembly

The national anthem of any country serves an essential purpose. The tune, taught in schools and known by all, boosts patriotism and serves as a beacon to focus attention and identity. Therefore, it requires a memorable introduction during a school assembly.

Therefore, it would be a good idea to make the words said before the national anthem, calling upon the crowd to sing in unison, worthy. So, what do you say when introducing the national anthem?

  • Our national anthem is the most valuable and respectable song for every citizen. Let's start it. Stand at ease, pay attention, stand straight, don't move and start the national anthem.
  • Ladies and gentlemen, we ask that you all please rise and direct your attention to the [American/Canadian/etc.] flag, located [give the location*], for playing our national anthem.
  • Mother and motherland are greater than heaven, so to show devotion towards motherland, all of you, please be ready in attention position for the national anthem.
  • The national anthem reminds us that we all are one and bonded with every word of it. Sing it with pride, sing it with a smile, sing it with joy, sing it with a heart full of love for your country.
  • The power of the national anthem unites us and fills our hearts with love for our country….. let us sing the national anthem.
  • Most people must learn the words of the National Anthem before they sing it. I learned these words when I was a child in elementary school, which have been embedded in me since I was an adult. Let’s rise for the national anthem.

How to ask the audience to stand for the national anthem

The national anthem is a patriotic song and deserves the utmost respect. So, when you co-ordinate the school morning assembly, it would be prudent to introduce the national anthem with the oomph it deserves. So, what do you say before singing the national anthem?

  • Good morning, everybody. It’s my distinct privilege for me to be here today. I am more than grateful to stand here and welcome you all to this ___________ (name of the seminar). Today I, ___________________ (your name), shall be walking you through the morning assembly. Before I start, I would like you to stand for the national anthem.
  • Good morning/good afternoon/ good evening, everyone. The galaxy of intellective. Honourable director, guests, parents, teachers, and our beloved students. How phenomenal is this day? You will witness an entertaining and amusing program ahead. It’s an honour to honour this charming and beautiful audience here. I, Ms………………, with my co-host Mr………………, once again welcome you all on behalf of the whole team and principal. Let us all rise for the national anthem.
  • We pray for your blessings with all humility – give us the wisdom to make this country a better place. To evoke feelings of patriotism and remind you of the nation’s glory and beauty, I request you all to stand up in respect to our national anthem.
  • Education is no allegation of knowledge but the formation of character. We roll down a carpet of welcome to all dignitaries, guests, parents, and invitees, well wishes and friends. Thank you all for giving us your precious time. Let us rise for the national anthem.
  • Hello!!!! And welcome!!!! My name is (name), and I welcome you to this grand event. The world is full of diamonds and gems, and we have some of them here today to make this event more beautiful. Let us rise for the national anthem.
  • I feel grateful to have the presence of our beloved principal, a person who is an inspiration to all. He has given us the path to follow to make our dreams a reality and given a firm foundation for all young minds. He is a role model for all the teachers and students for the discipline and incomparable hard work he practices daily. Let us stand and sing the national anthem.

National anthem introduction script

What is the best way to give command for the national anthem during a school assembly? One of the most creative ways is to use a script that will guide you and give you the most inspirational words when the moment comes. But how do you do it?

  • The opening line must be on point, and you should also welcome the guests appropriately. An example might come in handy the next time you are the assembly host.
  • Good morning/ good evening, everyone, on this spectacular day. The galaxy of thinkers and bookworms, honourable guests, dear teachers respected parents, and beloved students, how beautiful is this day. So, let us stand and sing the national anthem.
  • I take pride in getting this opportunity to welcome everyone to today’s morning assembly. Thus, I, (insert your name here), on behalf of our school head, Mr. (name of the principal), respected teachers and staff and my team, would like to give a gracious welcome to you all. Let’s start the assembly by singing the national anthem.
  • A pleasant morning/afternoon, ladies and gentlemen. Let us welcome the graduating class of School Year 2017-2018 at __________ high school together with their teachers, principals, division superintendent and guests. To start our program, please stand at attention for the national anthem (to be led by ______ Chorale, if there is any) under the baton of ___________. It will be followed by the singing of the ____ city/municipality hymn and prayer to be led by __________.
  • Good morning, ladies and gentlemen. A very happy and prosperous good morning to everyone present over here. My name is Yawar & today I will host today's morning assembly. It's my great pleasure & foremost duty on behalf of the patron. I assure you all that program shall be full of energy and enthusiasm & we all will get some beautiful memories out of it. But first, let us rise and sing the national anthem.
