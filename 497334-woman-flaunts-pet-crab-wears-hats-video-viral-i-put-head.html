  • In the heart of Nebraska, US, is a family that has adopted a Cardisoma armatum crab into the family
  • The pet crab is under the care of Laura Porter, who has impressed many with her undying love for the unconventional pet
  • She claimed the crab could do sign language, smile and loves eating peanut sandwiches, meatloaf and cheese

Laura Porter from Nebraska in the United States has impressed many on the internet with her pet rainbow crab, Howie, who she claimed can smile and use sign language.

Porter removes bacteria from Howie's shell

The stay-at-home mum said that people just loved her love for the pet. She also disclosed that editing videos of Howie is now a full-time job.

According to Mail Online, on March 8, the first video that she made removing bacteria from Howie's shell with a toothbrush got six million views.

The influencer also stuck an array of hats on the crab to humanise her, but several people who fear crabs said the creature looked like a giant spider instead.

Named after Howie Mandel

Porter bought Howie at an aquatic pet store in 2016 and her daughter named the crustacean after America's Got Talent's judge Howie Mandel, despite the clawed creature being female.

Howie is from a species known as Cardisoma armatum typically found in the wild in western Africa.

The family bought it after their daughter's fish died and it lives in a 55-gallon aquarium.

Porter described teaching Howie how to high five, saying the crab began to do it voluntarily while having her claws petted.

According to Porter, one of the crab's favourite past times was playing with her cat. She also loves meatloaf, peanut butter sandwiches and cheese.

Howie is fed with a lot of calories

The crab is fed with plenty of calories so she can reach maximum health.

"I avoid chocolate, fruit pits, acidic foods, spicy peppers, and onions," said the stay-at-home mum."I’ve lost count of the time that she’s molted. Howie’s actually reached her species maximum size for her gender,' Porter told Howie's followers in December," she added.

Moulting is the process in which crabs shed their shells and begin to grow another. Howie molts twice a year.

"They can die from molting. Molting is an extremely delicate process where she’s re-growing her mandibles, intestines and stomach lining. If she doesn’t separate from her molt properly, she will die. The last molt was very successful and I’m so happy," Porter said.
