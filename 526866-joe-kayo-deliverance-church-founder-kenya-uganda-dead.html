Joe Kayo: Deliverance Church Founder in Kenya, Uganda Is Dead

  • Apostle Joe Kayo is honoured as the founder of the Deliverance Church in both Kenya and Uganda
  • The celebrated servant of God is reported to have breathed his last on Thursday, November 2, while undergoing treatment at The Nairobi Hospital
  • Kayo leaves behind a wife and three children along with thousands of spiritual proteges, including Deliverance Umoja Bishop JB. Masinde

The founder of Deliverance Churches in Kenya and Uganda, Apostle Joe Kayo, is dead.

Deliverance church founder in Kenya

The revered preacher, referred to as God's general in his generation, breathed his last while undergoing treatment at the Nairobi Hospital on Thursday, November 2.

According to Joe Kayo Ministries, the pioneer of the Pentecostal-Charismatic Movement in East Africa had been unwell for some time.

Kayo, 86, leaves behind a wife and three children, along with thousands of spiritual sons and daughters he mentored.

Joe Kayo's proteges

Deliverance Church International, Umoja Bishop John Brown Masinde is one of Kayo's spiritual sons.

JB Masinde, as he is popularly known, was born again as a teenager when Kayo was preaching in Nairobi, and since then, he attributes his journey of spiritual growth to Kayo.

Who is Joe Kayo?

Kayo was born on May 5, 1937, to peasant parents in the then-Nyamira district, now a county, and unfortunately was orphaned at age 12.

He dropped out of school at Standard Six due to a lack of school fees and, after that, endured a tough childhood without parents, education, or basic needs.

With no hope for the future, Kayo sank into substance abuse and crime, and he even attempted to take his own life three times without success.

In 1957, in his prime teens, Kayo was hospitalised in a Mombasa hospital after he seriously fell ill.

With doctors having little hope for his recovery, they took him to televangelist T.L. Osborn's crusade and left him there, and it is when he heard about Jesus, accepted salvation, and was healed.

Life and Times of Joe Kayo

Two weeks later, while alone, Kayo revealed that he was baptised by the Holy Spirit and heard God's voice telling him to go and preach about the gospel.

With God's voice in 1957, the journey of Joe Kayo Ministries kicked off in coastal Kenya.

By 1960, he had established two congregations in Mombasa and Kilifi and moved to Nyanza, where he stayed with famous British preacher Derek Prince.

Kayo then moved to Kampala, Uganda, where, together with others, he pioneered the Pentecostal faith and stayed there for nine years. He later left Deliverance Church.

Kayo founded Deliverance Church Kenya and Uganda, Juba Pentecostal Church, and helped plant Family of God churches.

Kenyan pastors mourn Joe Kayo

Until his death, Kayo had weekly meetings with congregations at Nairobi City Hall.

In a Facebook post, Pastor Sarah Kinyanjui of the Sheerah City Builders Ministries eulogised Kayo as a great preacher who will be sorely missed and reminisced about the first time she visited his office.

Masinde, who earlier in the year urged spiritual fathers not to demand gifts from their daughters and sons and instead bless them, was full of praise for Kayo for giving him a solid spiritual foundation and always being there for him.
