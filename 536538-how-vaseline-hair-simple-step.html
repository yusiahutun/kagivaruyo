How to get Vaseline out of your hair in just 7 simple step

Are you looking for the best ways on how to get Vaseline out of your hair? Indeed, dealing with Vaseline in your hair can be a sticky situation. Whether it was accidental or an intentional beauty remedy gone wrong, removing Vaseline from your hair needs patience and the right approach.

Vaseline can benefit your hair but cause problems if not used correctly. For example, too much of it can damage your hair and make it look greasy. Additionally, Vaseline, an oil-based product, can clog your pores and cause flakiness, dandruff and itching on your scalp.

How to get Vaseline out of your hair in simple steps

If you're having trouble removing Vaseline from your hair, don't panic. There are several ways you can do it. Here are some of the most effective methods to quickly get Vaseline out of your hair overnight.

1. Clarifying shampoo

This is the most effective way of removing Vaseline from your hair. Clarifying shampoo is mainly designed to remove buildup and impurities from your hair.

Apply the shampoo, focusing on areas with too much Vaseline. Massage the shampoo to your scalp for several minutes, then rinse thoroughly using warm water. Repeat the process until Vaseline is entirely removed from your hair.

2. Corn starch or talc

Is it possible to get Vaseline out of hair without washing it? Yes, corn starch or talc are great options, as they absorb Vaseline. Apply enough cornstarch or talc and massage it into your hair and scalp. Leave it for some minutes before brushing it out. Repeat the process until the Vaseline is wholly removed.

3. Oil-based soap or glycerine soap

Oil-based soap or glycerine soap can dissolve Vaseline in your hair. First, wet your hair and apply soap to areas with Vaseline. Massage your hair and scalp for several minutes and rinse thoroughly using warm water. Repeat the steps until Vaseline is entirely removed from your hair.

4. Baby shampoo

Are you looking for the best way on how to get Vaseline out of baby hair? Well, baby shampoo is an excellent option. Apply a little amount of baby shampoo and massage it on your hair and scalp for a few minutes. Rinse thoroughly with warm water until Vaseline is removed from your hair.

5. Apple cider vinegar

Can apple cider vinegar to remove Vaseline from hair? Technically, any vinegar can do the job, but apple cider vinegar is among the best for hair.

For this method, try removing as much Vaseline from your hair as possible using paper towels or a regular towel. After this, apply the apple cider vinegar, gently work it into the hair, and wipe downward. Repeat this process until all Vaseline traces are gone. Lastly, wash your hair.

6. Eggs

It's not the best option, but whisked eggs can effectively remove Vaseline from your hair. Whisk together several eggs and use them as a shampoo, focusing on areas with the most Vaseline buildup. Gently wash your hair with warm water (though it's recommended to use something that smells nice to combat the egg smell).

7. Skin-safe adhesive remover

Skin-safe adhesive remover can effectively remove Vaseline from your hair. Apply adhesive remover on your hair and gently massage your scalp and hair for several minutes. Rinse your hair thoroughly and repeat the process until petroleum jelly is completely removed.

Note: Patch-testing new products before using them on your hair is recommended. If you experience severe reactions, stop using the particular product immediately and seek a dermatologist's advice.

What dissolves Vaseline?

Vaseline “petroleum jelly” can easily dissolve in fatty oil, ethyl ether, benzene, chloroform, carbon disulphide and turpentine but is difficult to dissolve in ethanol and is almost insoluble in water.
