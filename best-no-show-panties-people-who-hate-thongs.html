The 18 Best No-Show Panties of 2024

We all know the disappointment that comes with finding an extremely comfortable pair of underwear only to turn around and see lines showing through your jeans. And we think it's pretty safe to say no one wants that. So, to prevent that in the future, add a few pairs of no-show panties to your collection. We searched high and low (and consulted a bunch of experts) to find the best pairs that feel weightless and won't be detected under a pair of tight pants. We even found pairs for those who hate thongs because no, you don't have to wear a thong to be line-free.

We were impressed with pairs from brands like Skims, Parade, Bombas, Spanx, and more. Keep reading to see which ones made the cut.

What to Keep in Mind

Color

Click explains that there are three main components that people should keep in mind when searching for no-show panties: cut, color and texture. When it comes to color, Click says you need to first decide on whether you want your undergarments to match your skin tone or the garment you're wearing. "Don't assume your underpinnings should always match your skin tone," says Click. "Remember when Rihanna didn't wear a bedazzled thong to match her dress and regretted it?"

Cut

​​For cut, think about the hem and the fabric of your outfit. Are there any high hemlines or cut-outs to dodge? Skin tight garments, like leggings, probably would look best with a thong or cheeky cut underneath to help to avoid visible lines or wedgies (or at least a wedgie that wasn't intended to be there). “If you’re having a cozy at-home moment, try going for a brief — they’re great for jeans, trousers or more loose-fitting pieces,” recommends Rose Colcord, the founder of Cou Cou, an intimates label

Texture

Picking a seamless pair of undies with raw edges is a surefire way to ensure there are absolutely no panty lines. Jennifer Zuccarini, the founder and CEO of Fleur du Mal. warns about lace, which will "sometimes show through” because it typically looks raised, as well as metallic coating and any thickly ribbed bottoms.

Design

“It’s really tricky to point to just one thing when looking at design,” says Colcord. “Instead, I’d recommend purchasing your day-to-day undies from a brand that’s really putting care and attention into the fit of their pieces and also making sure that you’re buying the right size for you. No matter how seamless the undies, if you’re wearing the wrong size, they won’t be.”

But if you’re looking for some key details to look out for as you shop, Zuccarini has a few suggestions. “If it’s a seamless option, edges are either raw cut — which is unfinished fabric that is usually laser cut and will not fray — or bonded,” she says. “Bonded styles use fabric or a very fine elastic that is bonded on with heat instead of sewn together. It creates a very flat finish. You could wear a bikini cut or a cheeky or even full coverage, the key is to make sure it doesn’t dig in or ride up your butt, because that's what will show through your clothing.”

Your Questions, Answered

What fabric is best for no show panties?

“Typically, most people go for materials like polyamide or nylon that are often presented to be ‘seamless,’ however I recommend looking at the piece as a whole,” suggests Colcord. “The weight of the fabric, design and attention to quality of fit is what will ultimately dictate if your undies will be no-show. Plus, it’s also often overlooked that these materials aren’t optimal for gynecological health and also are quite harmful to the environment.” 

Colcord says people tend to go for polyamide or nylon, but materials to consider for breathability and stretch are elastane and cotton, but be sure they’re of good quality. Colcord recommends underwear crafted from organic cotton, for example, because it's “fully compostable and ideal for gynecological health. They’re seamless enough to wear under your silkiest slip dress, but also soft enough to wear all day everyday.” 

“I think a huge reason people typically go for the materials that are labeled as ‘seamless’ is because there’s this preconception that we can’t have it all - seamless, sustainable, comfy and cute - but, it’s really just about finding your perfect fit and finding brands that really place an importance on how they construct their pieces.”

How should you wash no show panties?

Because the fabric is so thin and delicate, no-show underwear could benefit from a hand wash. However, the time and effort that this takes might not be available to all. The next best thing is to throw all the pairs in a lingerie bag before tossing in your washing machine. The bag keeps the panties from getting tossed around or lost in the vortex of your machine. Be sure to wash your delicates in cold or room temperature water to preserve their elasticity.
