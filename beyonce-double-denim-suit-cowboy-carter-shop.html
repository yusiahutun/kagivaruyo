Beyonc Brings Back Double Denim Suiting for &quot;Cowboy Carter&quot;

Beyoncé’s country era is giving us dance, it’s giving us joy, and it’s giving us buckets of fashion inspiration.

For her latest Cowboy Carter–inspired fit, the superstar brought back the double denim trend of the early ’00s—but with a cowboy spin. The look was a blue denim set with a rinsed, vintage finish: a cropped Marine Serre jacket and high-waisted Y/Project jeans. The button-up jacket featured a corsetlike bodice, patchwork denim on the arms and bust—where a cone bra moment, à la Madonna, was the highlight—and a subtle print in the French brand’s signature moon motif. The pants were adorned with asymmetric diagonal cutout details in white along the front.

And the denim didn’t end there. Peeking out from under the pants were pointy denim pumps, and on Bey’s hand, a small version of Balenciaga’s Hourglass denim bag.

The singer finished the all-American look with a chic white Stetson cowboy hat, metallic-rim shades, a massive silver chain necklace, and a bold red lip. But her most shocking accessory of all was a giant American flag, which she even fashioned into a scarf at one point during her photo shoot—a clip of which she shared on Instagram. Now, while the U.S. flag has taken on a dark, Trump-y connotation since 2016 thanks to extremist Republicans, Beyoncé’s western era promises to take us back to non-ironic, non-problematic patriotism, evoking a time when country music was born from camaraderie amongst working-class, blue-collar Americans in the farms and city outskirts, their songs telling tales of finding moments of joy in everyday life.

The title of the singer’s upcoming album, Cowboy Carter, while of course a nod to her and husband Jay-Z’s last name, also calls to mind to the Carter Family, an iconic country music group made up of musician A.P. Carter’s relatives, popular from the 1930s through the 1950s.

“4 days until act ii COWBOY CARTER,” Beyoncé captioned her Instagram post featuring a slideshow of photos from a recent photo shoot. In some snaps, she also poses with a little rainbow flag and a Yuki No Bosha sake bottle.
