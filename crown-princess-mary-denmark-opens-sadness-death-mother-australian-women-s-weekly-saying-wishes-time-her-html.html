Crown Princess Mary of Denmark opens up about sadness over death of mother

Princess Mary of Denmark, famously born in Tasmania, has opened up about her late mother who passed when the royal was only 25-years-old.

Having just returned from a World Health Organisation mission, it was brilliant timing for the mother-of-four to front the cover of the latest Australian Women's Weekly. 

The 42-year-old spoke to the magazine about her absolute love for her mother and what she taught Mary from a young age.

Family first: Princes Mary of Denmark has spoken to the Australian Women's Weekly about her active role is bettering female equality

She's her mothers daughter: 'Mum definitely taught me to be independent and to trust myself'

A passionate spokesperson for women's rights, Princess Mary is set to fly off to South Africa to raise awareness of the tragic effects HIV has - especially on women and children.

This trip comes 17 years after the death of her late mother, Henrietta Clark Donaldson, who passed on the 20th of November.

Henrietta died at the young age of 55.

Her shocking death came after having heart surgery, leaving Mary without a mother at the impressionable age of 25. 

'I would love to have more time with my mother' Mary told the Australia Women's Weekly.

'She is part of me.'

Read all about it! Mary has spoken openly to The Australian Women's Weekly about her current projects and her late mother who inspired her

Special moment: Mary cradling her son and heir to the Danish throne Crown Prince Christian of Denmark in 2006

Living her dream: 'I would love to have more time with my mother'. Mary Christened her first born daughter Isabella Henrietta Ingrid Margrethe after her late mother in 2007

Henrietta never lived to see her daughter meet Prince Frederik and live out her fairy tale life.

Mary famously met the handsome prince in Sydney during the 2000 Olympics.

'Mum definitely taught me to be independent and to trust myself, and believe in myself and do whatever you set your mind to do. If you go after it, you can achieve it.' Mary told the Australia Women's Weekly.

Family holiday: Mary has four children to Prince Fredrik including Christian, Isabella and twins Vincent and Josephine. The couple made an official visit to Greenland in August this year taking all of their children

Motivating young women: A fortnight ago the princess attended a fitness run for children at the Arena School in Greve, Denmark

A role model for women: The Mary Foundation aims to support women and stop domestic violence

Her late mothers wise words obviously resonated deeply with the young royal who has gone on to lead many initiative that aim to empower other females and young girls.

Undoubtedly, the beautiful brunette is all about girl power.

She's the patron for the UN Population Fund and Maternity Worldwide and has her own organisation called The Mary Foundation which helps to stop domestic violence against women. 

Love at first sight: Mary famously met Prince Fredrik in Sydney during the 2000 Olympics. The couple met at the Slip Inn pub in Darling Harbour 

Wedding bells: Mary and Fredrik married four years after they first met on the 14th of May 2004 in Copenhagen 

Ten years on from marrying the man of her dreams and Mary has no intention to slow down or stop fighting for equality for women, making herself a fantastic role model for seven-year-old daughter Isabella and three-year-old Josephine.

'I's about giving them a secure happy environment where they can develop and grow and we can be together as a family.'

Princess Mary is currently in Tajikistan on the border of Afghanistan fulfilling her role as patron of the World Health Organisation.

She will be speaking at a conference about the dire health needs of women and children.  

Australian pride: Mary visited Australia in 2013 to commemorate the 40th anniversary of the Sydney Opera House which was famously designed by a Danish architect

Princess pals: Princess Mary and Catherine the Duchess of Cambridge share good luck, good nature and the rarity of marrying into a royal family. Mary gave Catherine a tour of the Denmark's UNICEF Emergency Supply Centre in 2011
