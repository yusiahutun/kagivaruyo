FEMAIL looks at what happened to the American Gladiators once the credits rolled

American Gladiators captivated audiences across the US as everyday heroes went up against muscle-bound marvels.

The show, which ran from 1989 to 1997, saw the cast catapulted into the limelight to become household names during their time on screen.

But what happened to the Gladiators themselves once the show went off air once and for all?

Many of the fan favorites went from strength-to-strength, carving out names for themselves in the TV and film industries or pursuing successful careers beyond the media world. 

American Gladiators captivated audiences across the US as everyday heroes went up against muscle-bound marvels. Pictured: The line-up in 1989

There were those, however, who were instead plagued by strife and tragedy.

Some were left battling crippling steroid addictions which saw them urinating blood and smuggling drugs.

And others tragically passed away prematurely - with two deaths reportedly linked to complications following surgeries. 

As Netflix unveils the sordid true story in its series Muscles & Mayhem: An Unauthorized Story of American Gladiators, FEMAIL takes a look at what happened to the athletes when the credits rolled.

 

Lee Reherman - Hawk: The star had carved out a name for himself in the media industry before tragically passing away aged 49

Lee Reherman, who was assigned the moniker of Hawk, quickly became a crowd favorite in the Gladiator arena with outstanding performances on the Powerball and Hang Tough events.

He stepped back from the show in 1998 but was able to capitalize on his popularity and forge a new path for himself in the media industry.

Reherman, from Kentucky, quickly racked up acting, hosting and voice over credits in more than 100 television and feature film projects.

Lee Reherman, who was assigned the moniker of Hawk, quickly became a crowd favorite in the Gladiator arena with outstanding performances on the Powerball and Hang Tough events

Tragically, in 2016, the much-loved star was found dead by his girlfriend at his house in Manhattan Beach, California, aged just 49

His extensive resume included a whole host of TV shows such as That's So Raven, The X-Files and Hannah Montana as well as film titles like Last Action Hero (1993) and Star Trek Into Darkness (2013).

But tragically, in 2016, the much-loved star was found dead by his girlfriend at his house in Manhattan Beach, California, aged just 49.

He had reportedly had hip replacement surgery a few weeks prior and had since complained about not feeling well. 

 

Dan Clark - Nitro: The athlete admitted to years of steroid abuse before surviving a heart attack 

Nitro, whose real name is Dan Clark, was considered to be the 'most volatile and outspoken member' of American Gladiators after his debut in 1989. 

After deciding to hang up his spandex for good, he turned his attentions to acting and featured in films including Death Becomes Her (1992) alongside Meryl Streep and Bruce Willis as well as Equilibrium (2002) with Christian Bale.

But he caused the biggest shock waves by releasing a tell-all memoir in 2009 titled Gladiator: A True Story of 'Roids, Rage, and Redemption in which he detailed his 20-year 'all-consuming addiction.'

Nitro, whose real name is Dan Clark, was considered to be the 'most volatile and outspoken member' of American Gladiators after his debut in 1989

His health took yet another hit in December 2013 after surviving a near-fatal heart attack which he has previously said ultimately 'gave him a second chance'

Clark, who was born in Zoma, Japan, detailed his battle with steroids which saw him urinating blood, smuggling drugs, smashing hotel rooms and even getting arrested.

His health took yet another hit in December 2013 after surviving a near-fatal heart attack which he has previously said ultimately 'gave him a second chance.' 

The star, who now counts yoga and marital arts among his hobbies, prides himself in helping others and recently founded the Gladiator Rock'n Run obstacle course which supports the Talk About Curing Autism charity. 

 

Sha-Ri Pendleton-Mitchell - Blaze: The athlete opted to become a substitute teacher but has also raised a very sporty son

Icelandic born Sha-Ri Pendleton-Mitchell, called Blaze on the show, was renowned for her aggression in the arena and excelled at the Conquer event.

The 5ft7in athlete starred as an American Gladiator from 1989 to 1992. 

Pendleton-Mitchell, who was accepted into law school and practiced criminal law, has since parlayed into the world of teaching.

Icelandic born Sha-Ri Pendleton-Mitchell, called Blaze on the show, was renowned for her aggression in the arena and excelled at the Conquer event

She now works as a substitute teacher in Bellflower, California, as well as being member of the track coaching team

She now works as a substitute teacher in Bellflower, California, as well as being member of the track coaching team, according to Sports Illustrated.

Her son Re-Al, whom she shares with Knights and Warriors' Rodney Mitchell, has proven his own credentials as a sportsman.

He reportedly excels as a 100 and 200 meters sprinter as well as a football quarterback. 

 

William 'Billy' Smith - Thunder: He returned to his body-building roots but sadly died at the age of 56 

William 'Billy' Smith proved to be an instant hit after joining American Gladiators as Thunder for the show's second season in 1990.

The blonde behemoth, who was known for entering the arena on a Harley-Davidson motorcycle, initially returned to his body-building roots after his stint on the show came to an end.

He returned to the USA Championships in 1995 at the age of 30 and secured fifth place - but it would turn out to be his last contest.

William 'Billy' Smith proved to be an instant hit after joining American Gladiators as Thunder for the show's second season in 1990

In 1992, Smith became the founder, president and CEO of Intrafitt which offers individualized nutrition and exercise program

Instead, he turned his attention toward the scientific discipline of diet and exercise in an attempt to develop a plan that could enhance his brother's lifestyle after he was diagnosed with Type 1 Diabetes. 

As a result, in 1992, Smith became the founder, president and CEO of Intrafitt which offers individualized nutrition and exercise program.

Sadly, the entrepreneur, who lived in North Carolina, died in 2021 at the age of 56. It was reported that he had developed an infection following knee surgery but no official cause of death was ever confirmed.

 

Lori Fetrick - Ice: The blonde beauty fell into depression after being fired from the show and she turned her back on athletics to enter the world of finance 

California-born athlete Lori Fetrick, who was assigned the name Ice, joined the American Gladiators line-up for five seasons. 

Her time on the show came to an abrupt end after being fired - along with Nitro and two other Gladiators - by creator and producer Sam Goldwyn after refusing to return to filming in a dispute over money from merchandise.

The situation saw her battling depression, according to the new Netflix documentary, but she soon got back on her feet and initially went to work as a personal trainer in Los Angeles before opening her own gym in Orlando, Florida.

California-born athlete Lori Fetrick, who was assigned the name Ice, joined the American Gladiators line-up for five seasons

She now works as a financial professional at Transamerica and 'travels the country conducting seminars on health and financial wellness'

The former Gladiator has since turned her hand to a whole host of ventures including television and film appearances, co-authoring a book, producing a fitness video and even launching her own line of T-shirts - but Fetrick soon took an altogether different path.

She now works as a financial professional at Transamerica and 'travels the country conducting seminars on health and financial wellness,' according to Sports Illustrated. 

Additionally, she continues to host her podcast titled Chillin' With Ice, where she has more than 1,600 subscribers, on which she interviews her former Gladiators costars.

 

Jim Starr - Laser: The household name pursued a series of business ventures before opting to work in product development

Jim Starr, better known as Laser, holds the title for being the longest-serving male American Gladiator after starring in all seven series of the classic format.

And it seems that the show is in his blood after being intertwined with the production long after it went off air.

Starr, who lives in California, is still a regular at several of NBC's American Gladiators events.

Jim Starr, better known as Laser, holds the title of being the longest-serving male American Gladiator after starring in all seven series of the classic format

The athlete is also the owner of his own company, Sports Nutrition Consulting Group, and recently became a grandfather

In his personal life, Starr recently became a grandfather - posting a slew of pictures holding his new grandson on social media

He continued in the fitness theme and became the Director of Product Development at Life Time Fitness - a Minnesota-based chain of high-end gyms - before taking up the same position at hunting brand The Grind.

The athlete is also the owner of his own company, Sports Nutrition Consulting Group.

In his personal life, Starr recently became a grandfather - posting a slew of pictures holding his new grandson on social media. 

 

Lynn 'Red' Williams - Sabre: The American Gladiator is now a grandfather-of-three and teaches scripture

Lynn 'Red' Williams had been a member of Bloods gang during high school in Los Angeles before finding his feet among the Gladiator cast in 1992.

He has often credited alter-ego Sabre as being a role model for children in his community - and believes his appearance on the show had a positive impact.

After eventually stepping back, Williams was able to transfer his athleticism into a starring role as Jax in 1997's video game movie sequel Mortal Kombat: Annihilation alongside fellow Gladiator Malibu (Deron McBee).

Lynn 'Red' Williams (left) had been a member of Bloods gang during high school in Los Angeles before finding his feet among the Gladiator cast in 1992

He is also a member of the Constitutional Rights Foundation and the Cancer II Foundation and now teaches scripture in the San Pedro, California, area

And he went on to reprise the role for the follow-up, Mortal Kombat: Federation of Martial Arts, released in 2000. 

But beyond his on-screen prowess, the star has carved out time for his family - having had two children and three grandchildren.

He is also a member of the Constitutional Rights Foundation and the Cancer II Foundation and now teaches scripture in the San Pedro, California, area.

 

Galen Tomlinson - Turbo: The keen sportsman became a volleyball coach and helped his niece make the USA National Team

Galen Tomlinson, from California, was often referred to as 'the best athlete on the team' after joining American Gladiators as Turbo in 1990.

Like many of his former costars, Tomlinson easily found work in the TV industry after leaving the show - with credits including Amen, Family Matters and Who's The Boss.

But the keen sportsman wanted to continue to pursue his passions and became Head Coach and Director of Coast North County Volleyball Club in San Diego.

Galen Tomlinson, from California, was often referred to as 'the best athlete on the team' after joining American Gladiators as Turbo in 1990

The keen sportsman wanted to continue to pursue his passions and became Head Coach and Director of Coast North County Volleyball Club in San Diego

With more than 15 years of experience in the field, he helped daughter Kourtney and niece Carli Lloyd excel at the sport.

Tomlinson had helped raise Carli after her father died when she was just four years of age.

And she has since gone on to become a respected member of the USA National Team - and won bronze alongside her teammates at the 2016 Rio Olympic Games.
