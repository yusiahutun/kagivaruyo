Grammys 2022: Ledisi stuns in a flowing gold dress while posing with her husband Ronald T. Young

Ledisi and her husband, Ronald T. Young, attended the 64th annual Grammy Awards at the MGM Grand Garden Arena in Las Vegas on Sunday afternoon.

The 50-year-old singer and her spouse stayed close while posing for a photo on the star-studded event's red carpet, and the performer also stuck around for a set of solo shots while enjoying the spotlight.

The performer has been nominated for the Grammy Award for Best Traditional Pop Vocal Album for her work on her recently released album, Ledisi Sings Nina.

Power couple: Ledisi and her husband, Ronald T. Young, attended the 64th annual Grammy Awards at the MGM Grand Garden Arena in Las Vegas on Sunday afternoon

Ledisi wore an eye-catching gold dress that flowed gracefully while she posed for the cameras.

The singer-songwriter also donned numerous articles of gold jewelry and she sported a reflective belt at her midriff during the event.

Ledisi's first Grammy nomination came in 2008, when she was put up for the Best New Artist award, which went to Amy Winehouse.

She was also put up for the Best R&B Album award that year, although she ultimately came up short. 

The performer received the same nomination in 2010 for her work on Turn Me Loose, and she was also given a chance to win the Best Female R&B Vocal Performance award.

In the past: Ledisi's first Grammy nomination came in 2008, when she was put up for the Best New Artist award, which went to Amy Winehouse

However, the distinctions that year went to Maxwell and Beyonce, respectively. 

Ledisi was subsequently nominated for the Grammy Awards for Best R&B Performance and Best R&B Song for her work on Pieces of Me in 2012.

She also received a nomination for Best R&B Album for her album of the same name, although she did not win any of the distinctions that year.

The singer-songwriter was later put up for the Grammy Award for Best R&B Performance in 2012, 2013 and 2015.

2018 saw Ledisi receive nominations for the awards for Best Traditional R&B Performance and Best R&B Album for All The Way and Let Love Rule, respectively.

Finally! The performer won her first award during last year's ceremony, when she was given the Best Traditional R&B Performance distinction for her track Anything For You

The performer won her first award during last year's ceremony, when she was given the Best Traditional R&B Performance distinction for her track Anything For You.

The singer went up against artists Mykal Kilgore and Chloe x Halle for the award.

Ledisi has since been nominated for the Grammy Award for Best Traditional Pop Vocal Album for her work on Ledisi Sings Nina. 

The album serves as a tribute to the artistic output of prolific singer Nina Simone, and the performer collaborated with Metropole Orkest on several of the tracks.

Other artists who have been nominated for the award are Tony Bennett and Lady Gaga, Willie Nelson, Dolly Parton, Norah Jones and Tori Kelly.

New output: Ledisi has since been nominated for the Grammy Award for Best Traditional Pop Vocal Album for her work on Ledisi Sings Nina

Music's biggest night recognizes the best recordings, compositions, and artists of the eligibility year, which runs from September 1, 2020, to September 30, 2021.

This year, composer and musical artist Jon Batiste surpassed all nominees with an impressive 11 nominations, while Justin Bieber, Doja Cat and H.E.R. have eight each and Billie Eilish and Olivia Rodrigo tied with seven. 

The Recording Academy has eliminated nominations review committees this year meaning winners will now be determined by a majority vote from Recording Academy voting members.

There are famously a long list of categories - 88 to be exact. 

The Grammys were initially set to take place on Jan. 31 in Los Angeles, but got pushed due to the rapid spread of the Omicron variant of the coronavirus.

'Given the uncertainty surrounding the Omicron variant, holding the show on January 31 simply contains too many risks,' CBS and the Recording Academy said.   

Harvey Mason Jr., CEO of the Recording Academy, confirmed later in January that it would be moving to Las Vegas.
