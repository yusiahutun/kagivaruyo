Tom Ray was left disfigured and without arms and legs after a battle with sepsis

A man whose life was left hanging in the balance after contracting sepsis has revealed how he and his wife initially dismissed his illness as food poisoning.

Tom Ray, now 54, was struck down with the deadly blood poisoning at the age of 38, after doctors believe a dentist caught his gum during a routine appointment. 

Ray, from Oakham in Rutland, appeared on This Morning today to tell how the illness put him in a coma for five months and how he awoke having lost both his arms and legs and with facial disfigurement.

Tom and Nic, who have two teenage children together, pictured on their wedding day in 1998. Nic was 8 and a half months pregnant with the couple's second child, Freddy, when Tom fell ill 

He appeared alongside his wife Nic, also 54, and told This Morning hosts Holly Willoughby and Ben Shephard that sepsis had 'destroyed' his family's life. 

Later this month, a new film, Starfish, is released which documents the couple's heartbreaking story. 

Starring Tom Riley and Joanne Froggatt, the film retells the family's recovery from the tragedy and how they've pieced their lives back together, albeit in an entirely different way.

The tragedy occurred when Tom was 38 and the couple were just weeks away from the birth of their second child, Freddy.

When Tom complained of cold hands and a headache, the couple assumed he was merely suffering from food poisoning or a winter bug. 

However, a night of vomiting followed and despite rallying temporarily the following day after being prescribed anti-nausea drugs, Tom's condition dramatically deteriorated. 

Nic returned home from a family visit to find her husband shivering with cold, blue lipped and starting to lose consciousness.

Sepsis, initially dismissed as food poisoning, robbed Tom of his limbs and left his face disfigured after doctors were forced to cut away damaged flesh around his nose and mouth

Tom initially believed that he'd been struck down with food poisoning after eating some out-of-date sausages but his condition quickly deteriorated and he was left in a coma that lasted for two months

Tom says he would lie awake in the hospital trying to rationalise what had happened to him and even invented a car crash as a way of justifying to himself how he lost his arms and legs

Tom and Nic chatted to Ben and Holly about Starfish, the new film about their family's struggle after Tom's illness

What followed is every family's worst nightmare; a fraught ambulance ride to hospital where doctors pumped him with antibiotics in a battle to save him. 

Tom told Ben and Holly: 'I just started feeling incredibly ill, very confused, very sick with a very high temperature and feeling like I was going to die. I woke up in a coma five months later.'

WHAT IS SEPSIS? 

Sepsis, or blood poisoning, is a reaction to an infection that causes the body to damage its own organs and tissues. 

If not spotted and treated quickly, it can rapidly lead to organ failure and death. 

Its key symptoms include: 

  • Slurred speech, which is triggered by a lack of blood supply to the brain. 
  • Mottled or discoloured skin anywhere on the body. 
  • Extremely painful muscles due to a lack of oxygen. 
  • Passing no urine in one day, as the kidneys stop working properly. 
  • Severe breathlessness. The body senses there isn’t enough oxygen getting to the brain, so it increases the ‘drive’ to breathe to increase it. 
  • Chronic tiredness and swelling of the affected area. 

This year the government announced funding for a campaign by the Sepsis Trust to improve public awareness of sepsis, empowering people to ‘Just Ask: Could It Be Sepsis?’ 

Earlier recognition of the condition could save 14,000 lives each year in the UK. 

For more information, visit sepsistrust.org

Tom missed the birth of Freddy while he battled for survival in a coma. His legs were amputated at the knee and his arms at the elbow. 

His wife says she lived minute-to-minute in the following weeks: 'Your life just collapses and reduces down to the next 15 minutes, next half hour, next hour, next day. 

'Every hour you're bombarded with information and decisions, because he's not in a position to make any decisions.'

 'Your life just collapses and reduces down to the next 15 minutes, next half hour, next hour, next day. Nic Ray on the days after Tom contracted sepsis 

When he awoke, Nic had to explain to Tom on multiple occasions what had happened and Tom says he would lie awake at night trying to rationalise what had occurred.

He said: 'I invented a memory of a car crash and decided I must have been in a car crash.' 

He continued: 'I went home and tried to get on terms with my children, it was very difficult for them to look at me.'

Nic told Ben and Holly that despite Tom once being the main caregiver at home and spending almost everyday with his daughter, she was initially scared of the way he looked. 

She explains: 'All she could do was talk about seeing Tom. She took one look at him and freaked. She ran away and hid. We found her underneath an NHS chocolate machine.'

The family say Tom's disability 'destroyed' their lives and they were forced to sell their house. Tom is now back in work with a 'very understanding' employer. 

Such is the scarring to Tom's face that Nic hasn't been able to kiss her husband since. Despite years of operations to rebuild his face, Tom remains disfigured.

A moment in the new film reveals how the couple haven't kissed since Tom's illness

Tom and his family say their story has now come 'full circle' with the film about their lives

In a recent interview with You Magazine, Nic revealed: 'We haven’t kissed since it happened.'

Starfish features moments that offer insight into how hard it's been for the couple to re-unite as a couple including a scene where he reaches across to his wife in bed and she tells him: 'I’ll never stop loving you, Tom, never stop believing in you. 

'But I can’t make love to you. I can’t be that girl any more. I’ve seen, done, things no lover should ever have to.' 

The couple say they're delighted that after ten years of being in the pipeline, the film is finally seeing the light of day. 

Tom told Ben and Holly: 'It's unbelievable. We both started out in the creative industry so everything has come full circle. We're living the dream.'
