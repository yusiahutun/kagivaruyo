Is THIS the secret behind Iam Tongi's American Idol win? How Hawaiians rallied behind singer

He's solidified himself as the pride of Hawaii after being crowned this year's American Idol.

Iam Tongi - an 18-year-old high school student from Kahuku, Oahu - beat out fellow teens Colin Stough and Megan Danielle to become the winner of the ABC singing competition.

Over the weeks leading up to Sunday's finale, the teen and his supporters have been able to rally fans across the islands and the US to help push his popularity to new heights.

Even Josh Green, the governor of Hawaii, urged fans to vote for Tongi, whose powerful voice and tragic back story won the hearts of viewers everywhere.

Iam Tongi solidified his status as the pride of Hawaii when he won this year's American Idol

Iam Tongi, an 18-year-old high school student from Kahuku, Hawaii, beat out fellow teens Colin Stough and Megan Danielle (pictured with Tongi)

Tongi's father Rodney died just months before the teen's American Idol audition.

Following Rodney's passing, Tongi, who grew up in Hawaii, and his family were forced to move to Washington's Federal Way after they were 'priced out of paradise.'

When he was named in the top three, Tongi received a hero's welcome with a parade and 3,000 people gathering at Turtle Bay Resort to hear him sing live with shuttle buses ferrying people to the concert, KHON2 reported at the time.

During his homecoming, the 18-year-old's former school, Kahuku High School, gave him with an honorary diploma, according to Kauai Now.

On TikTok, the Iam Tongi hashtag has seen almost 300 million views.  

And in his weekly update on social media, just days before the American Idol finale, the governor of Hawaii reminded people to vote for Tongi.

'Iam Tongi is lighting up the world, making us proud. Beautiful, heartfelt voice, soulful,' Green said.

'Vote for Iam Tongi. He's our next American Idol.'

Honolulu business Waikiki Trolley shared a TikTok video that gave a glimpse into the kind of support Tongi had in Hawaii.

Roadside signs and supporters for the 18-year-old, who has Tongan and Samoan heritage, were seen out in force in the clip.

And their efforts paid off. 

For the first time in American Idol's 20-year history, a Hawaiian was named as its winner, sparking fans to flood social media celebrating the milestone.

'Congratulation, Iam!! You were amazing tonight and deserve all of this! So happy to have been in the studio to cheer you on and celebrate this moment! Keep making Hawaii proud! All my love and aloha,' one person wrote on Instagram.

Another added: 'The fact that you continued to represent your father, family, culture, and the people of the pacific from the beginning audition to the finale just goes to show how much you love who you are! The people of the pacific have been behind you since day 1 and will continue to be behind you on this journey! Yessahh.'

'All of Hawaiʻi, Polynesia, and the world is celebrating you,' a third comment read, adding: 'Your dad is smiling down upon you! Only the beginning of bigger blessings awaiting you.' 

'Iam Tongi brought the whole polynesian community together. I'm telling y'all, we're more powerful TOGETHER,' a Twitter user wrote after the result was announced.

'The islands are celebrating your win tonight Iam! Congratulations! Well deserved. Your dad is smiling down from heaven and so proud of you,' someone else said.

Others said Tongi's presence got them and their loved ones watching the show.

'Iam is just… really special. He motivated my 19 year old son, who is far from the typical viewing demographic of this show, to pull out his phone and vote tonight. Even my older son who is a music student and does nothing but critique every contestant's technique when he passes through the room agreed that FINALLY, the right contestant won. Iam, you deserve the world!!' one redditor said.

'I've never voted on idol before , but you inspired me to believe in someone else dream... if I could get another day with my dad... Congratulations!!! Hawaii here he comes!!!!' another Instagram comment read.

Some American Idol fans were also happy to see that this year's winner wasn't another country singer.

'Seems like some Country fans are upset that one of the two Country finalists didn't win this year. I say "THIS" year because Country has won out the past 3 years. Iam is a refreshing change from all the twang and whining! GOD BLESS YOU, IAM!' one person said.

A Twitter user simply wrote: 'So happy he beat out the country singers.'

On reddit, one comment read: 'Country idol was no match!!! Congrats Iam!!'
