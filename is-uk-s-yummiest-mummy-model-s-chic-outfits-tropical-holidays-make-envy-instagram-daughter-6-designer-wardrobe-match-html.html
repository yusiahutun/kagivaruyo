London mother Hannah Strafford Taylor gains Instagram fame for super chic outfits

Most mothers cart the kids off to school in their pyjamas, with their hair scraped into a messy bun and half of their offspring's breakfast in their hair - but not Hannah Strafford Taylor.

Indeed, the mother-of-two has earned herself quite the reputation (and social media following) for her super chic designer ensembles and effortless sense of style that she loves to match with her mini-me daughter.

Hannah, 37, a full-time mother from London, has a penchant for designer clothes and it seems her daughter, Soleil, six, is already catching on. 

The pair often take mother-daughter snaps for social media in co-ordinating designer outfits - and with three-month-old Winter Delphina recently being born, it's only a matter of time before the trio start matching their wardrobes.

Speaking about her passion for fashion, Hannah, who has been with her finance CEO husband since she was 24, said: 'I have always been obsessed with clothes, even at a young age, I spent hours in my mother's wardrobe creating outfits with silk scarfs.

'I guess I started curating my wardrobe properly when I moved to London to model when I was 18. As soon as I got paid I would jump on the bus to Harvey Nichols and spend everything I had on something from Chloe or Gucci, then I would live on pasta and pesto as I had no money for food!

'Later on I started buying and selling clothes, I would buy pieces that were limited and then sell them on eBay for a profit.'

In her late 20s, Hannah worked as 'one of those headless models' at Net-a-porter and got to see the latest designer garments before they went online. 'Put it this way - I never made any money from that job!,' she joked.

Hannah cites her favourite brands as Chanel - but acknowledges that it is 'crazy expensive' so tries to choose items that will stand the test of time - and e-retailers such as Browns Fashion, Farfetch and Vestiaire Collective  Despite her taste for the finer things in life, she still champions M&S and Zara, too.

Sharing her number one tip for anyone hoping to emulate her maternity style, she said: 'Keep it simple! Wear what suits you. Everyone looks different in pregnancy so wear what flatters you rather than going with crazy trends. 

'I tried to wear clean lines and accentuate the bump in some way, using a belt above my bump or wearing a long dress with a tie. You also want to be comfortable, no point wobbling around in crazy high heels!''

Hannah spends her day cooking - which is her passion - or running around Marylebone doing errands or grabbing coffee with her mother. Then it is back to the school run and more 'mummy duties.'

'I do go to the odd event or lunch, but honesty most of my time is spent doing what I love most, being a mum,' she said.

Hannah also spends plenty of time abroad and is a self-proclaimed sun-worshiper. 

'I love a holiday,' she admits. 'My husband says I have my own unique currency of holidays. I compare everything to the price of a holiday.

'My favourite place in the world is the Maldives , I wish we could go all the time, I love the tranquility and the beauty, I always feel so healthy when we are there; it really is sheer paradise.

'We used to spend a lot of time in St Tropez, as we had a villa there, but we sold that last year so am looking forward to trying new places.'

So has Hannah ever been insulted for spending a lot on clothes and, if so, how would she respond?

'Only my husband has insulted me and I tell him I am constantly selling things to pay for the new, which I honestly do.'
