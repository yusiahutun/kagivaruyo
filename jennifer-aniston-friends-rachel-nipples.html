Jennifer Aniston on Why Her Nipples Kept Popping up on 'Friends'

Jennifer Aniston's Friends character Rachel Green was all over the #freethenipple campaign super early. And we love her for it. Fans have been wondering why her nipples are so prominent in the show—or if it was an accident—and JenAn was happy to weigh in.

Everybody loves Friends, and Rachel Green was the ultimate trendsetter. Thanks to Rachel's incredible hair, amazing fashion, and transformation from rich daddy's girl to high-flying fashion buyer, fans were kind of obsessed with everything she did. (If only she ditched that whiny Ross on the season finale and jetted out to Paris....)

But there's one thing that's left fans amused about Rachel over the years and that's why her nipples made so many appearances. Not that it's a bad thing—they were freaking legendary—but she was basically the OG for the #freethenipple movement.

Getty

Fans have come up with their own theories. Some have said they think the wardrobe team did a Mean Girls and cut holes in Jennifer's bras to make her nipples show through (really?), while others assumed it was just a personal choice the actor made behind the scenes. Or maybe it wasn't even a conscious decision? One fan tweeted in 2015: "Did you know that Friends' producers told Rachel and Monica to cut holes in their bras to show their nipples?"

Other fans believed it was Jennifer who insisted on taking a pair of scissors to her outfits. Someone else tweeted, "You do know she made wardrobe cut holes in her bras so nips would show."

Over the years, Jennifer Aniston has finally answered the burning question, and unsurprisingly, her answer is flawless.

Asked by Vogue about it in August 2017, the actor explained there was no real rhyme or reason to it, but she is proud of the way her boobs are. AMEN.

"Yeah I don't know what to say about that!" Jennifer said. "It's just one of those things, I guess. I wear a bra. I don't know what to tell ya! And I don't know why I'm supposed to be ashamed of them—it's just the way my breasts are. But hey, OG, I'm not going to complain!"

David Hume Kennerly//Getty Images

Kudos to Jen for being so candid; We all have nipples, and unsurprisingly they don't all come in the same shape and size, but it's nice to be reminded of that by the ultimate Hollywood star. Glad that's finally been cleared up. Or should we say...nipped in the bud?
