Kate wears green Balmain blazer and white skirt at Wimbledon

One of the most celebrated events of the British summer season, Wimbledon always sees celebrities and royals showcase excellent outfits. Today was no different: the Princess of Wales arrived in SW19 in a look that spoke to both practical summer dressing (as the rain came down thick and fast) and 1980s power suiting.

Kate, who is a keen tennis fan and attends Wimbledon every year in her role as patron of the All England Lawn Tennis Club (AELTC), was there to watch British number one Katie Boulter play Australia's Daria Saville. Last week, before the tournament began, she appeared in a heartwarming video to celebrate Wimbledon's ball boys and girls alongside tennis champion Roger Federer, showcasing her own skills on the court.

Neil Mockford//Getty Images

For her latest outing, the royal referenced the typically all-white Wimbledon uniform with her crisp, pleated midi-skirt, which she teamed with a preppy, mint green blazer with exaggerated white lapels and buttons by Balmain. She topped off the look with a matching white bag by Mulberry, and her favourite white Gianvito Rossi pumps.

Pool//Getty Images

Bold blazers, overstated lapels and white heels are synonymous with 1980s fashion; a very similar look was, in fact, worn by Kate's mother-in-law, Princess Diana, to Trooping the Colour in 1988, for which she sported a green and white coat dress by Catherine Walker – co-ordinating with a young Prince Harry. Kate and Diana even chose the same jewellery, both opting for pearls, an '80s favourite. Kate's earrings are the 'Synthea' style from Shyla, which are still available to buy below.

Anwar Hussein//Getty Images

It's not the first time in recent weeks that the princess has paid homage to this particular outfit of Diana's. In June, Kate wore vibrant green Andrew Gn outfit with a wide-brimmed Philip Treacy hat for Trooping the Colour, a choice which also seemed to directly reference Diana's look on the famous Buckingham Palace balcony.

Samir Hussein//Getty Images

The action at Wimbledon is set to continue throughout the fortnight, but there are a number of ways you can join in on the action – without leaving your sofa. Earmark Zendaya's new film, Challengers, which is out in September and promises plenty of on-court drama, as well as the upcoming TV series Fifteen-Love, starring Aidan Turner, which is set in the cut-throat world of professional tennis.
