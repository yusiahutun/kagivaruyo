Early Life

Kendra Westerhaus was born in July 1982 in State College, Pennsylvania, and currently resides in Pocatello, Idaho. She pursued her B.A. from Case Western Reserve University, majoring in Psychology. Mark and JoAnne Westerhaus are her parents while Briton Westerhaus is her brother and she has also a sister whose name is not revealed. Kendra grew up in State College but later shifted to Pocatello in order to pursue Ph.D. in 2005. From 2005 to 2008, she pursued her Masters of Science (M.S.) in clinical psychology from Idaho State University. Later, she had her doctorate in clinical psychology from the same university. Kendra’s parent’s name is Mark and Joanne. She was a foosball champ in her college and was undefeated with a score of 1-0. She calls herself a retired foosball champion.

Kendra Westerhaus Career

Kendra began her professional life in September 2010 while interning with “The Guidance Center” in Long Beach. From September 2011 to September 2013, she worked as a service extender/psychologist under the supervision of “Psychological Assessment Specialists,” based in the Pocatello, Idaho Area.

She worked as a Licensed Clinical Psychologist for “Psychological Assessment Specialists” from September 2011 to November 2018. From January 2018 to May 2021, she worked as Adjunct Professor at Idaho State University. Most recently, she has worked as Child Psychologist, and Child/Pediatric Team Lead for HEALTH WEST, INC. since November 2018.
