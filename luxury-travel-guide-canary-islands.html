Your luxury travel guide to the Canary Islands

When it comes to luxury travel, there's no need to fly thousands of miles when you can get the same experience practically on your doorstep. The Canary Islands, off the coast of North Africa, are just a few hours away from the UK and have a balmy, year-round climate, incredible beaches, superb gastronomy and, of course, some of the best hotels.

What to see

El Hierro: Despite being the smallest of the seven main Canary Islands, El Hierro is chock full of natural wonders and richly deserving of its Unesco World Biosphere status. Covered in lush vegetation, with dramatic cliffs and dense forests, the island has 46 dive sites, making it one of the best places in the Canaries to explore the spectacular marine life. For breathtaking views, head up to the Punta de la Orchilla on the south west of the island, one of Spain's most remote lighthouses.

Fuerteventura: With more than 150km of golden sandy beaches, impressive sand dunes and sparkling azure seas, Fuerteventura looks and feels like a tropical paradise. If you're looking for total peace and quiet, the Isolote de Lobos nature reserve, an islet just north of Fuerteventura, is a short boat ride away and full of diverse flora and fauna, including rare bird species.

Getty Images

You'll feel like a kid in a candy store trying to choose which beach to visit - but for sheer jaw-dropping quality, Cofete is hard to beat. Twelve kilometers long and with no development to speak of, it's as wild and untamed as they come. At the other end of the spectrum, Playa de Los Charcos is the place to head if you want to be able to stroll out of the sea and straight into a luxury hotel or upmarket seafood restaurant.

Lanzarote: Formed out of black volcanic rock, Lanzarote's landscape is extraordinary. In the south of the island, a five-minute drive from the popular Playa Blanca, the natural rock pools of Los Charcones are an ideal place to kick back and relax - or you might want to dive into the inviting indigo waters of the deeper ones.

Charco del Palo is for the adventurous of spirit. A naturist holiday village on Lanzarote's north-eastern, it's built around a rocky coastline and you're free to strip off just about everywhere apart from shops and restaurants. Caleta de Famara is one of the best sandy spots on Lanzarote. A laid-back seaside village with a young, bohemian vibe, it offers surfers some of the most thrilling breaks in Europe.

Tenerife: Benijo beach near the town of Taganana is a big draw for nature lovers and naturists alike. With fabulous views of the Roques de Anaga rock formations, the sunsets are here are something to behold. There are plenty of tavernas and restaurants nearby where you can try authentic Tenerife cuisine.

What to do

Getty Images

The Canary Islands' volcanic legacy and balmy climate make it a haven for sports and outdoor activities of all kinds, including kitesurfing, trekking, mountain-biking, birdwatching and even dolphin and whale spotting. And if you travel with children, the volcanic islands provide many opportunities for education as well as fun.

Take a hike through one of the islands' many national parks, such as Timanfaya on Lanzarote or Teide on Tenerife, where the fantastical lava formations makes it a favourite of sci-fi directors, who have used it as the backdrop for Hollywood movies such as Clash Of The Titans and One Million Years BC.

Thanks to its cloudless skies, the Canary Islands is one of the best places in the world to observe the night skies, which are legally protected to prevent light and radio wave pollution and control flight patterns. Tenerife, La Palma and Fuerteventura all have world-class observatories where you can take guided stargazing tours.

What to eat

With influences from Europe, Africa and beyond, rich volcanic soil and a mild climate, Canary Islands cuisine provides rich pickings, with an abundance of seafood, wines, cheeses and fruit, such as the famous Canarian banana.

Gran Canaria rum, Malvasia wine from Lanzarote, goats cheese from Fuerteventura, palm honey from La Gomera, quesadilla cheese cake from El Hierro and Tenerife Heirloom potatoes, eaten fried and crinkly with spicy Canarian mojo sauce are just a few must-try items.

How to relax

Wellness tourism in the Canary Islands goes back to the Victorians, who came in their droves to take advantage of the mineral-rich springs, the temperate climate and beautiful scenery. Today, there are luxury spas and wellness retreats galore, while exercise junkies can get their fix with beach yoga sessions, beach volleyball, paddle boarding and, of course, swimming.

Where to stay

Fuerteventura

Fuerteventura has some of the best beaches of the Atlantic - 150km of golden sand and crystal clear waters. There are three Iberostar Resorts and Hotels resorts on Jandia beach in the south of the island, making it the ideal destination for couples looking for a relaxing break, as well as families with children.

There are two incredible hotels on striking, volcanic Lanzarote: the three-star Iberostar Lanzarote Park Hotel, a comfortable apart-hotel catering to families and the four-star Iberostar La Bocayna Village Hotel, a beachfront resort where you're guaranteed total R&R.

Tenerife

With plenty of four-and-five-star hotels in some of Tenerife's most sought-after locations - the Iberostar Grand Hotel Mencey in the charming city of Santa de la Cruz and the adults-only Iberostar Grand Hotel El Mirador in scenic Costa Adeje, for example - you're bound to find an option that suits your requirements and budget.
