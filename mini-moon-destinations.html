The best mini-moon destinations

No 131, the Cotswolds

A beautifully restored Georgian townhouse in the heart of Cheltenham, this quirky hotel is made up of 11 individually furnished bedrooms, a restaurant serving seasonal English food and a bar with an extensive cocktail list. Your stay will feel like spending time in a home away from home. From £150 a night (no131.com).

The Mazot at Chalet Perdu, French Alps

This little alpine lodge, located in the Les Gets resort, is the perfect romantic escape for those who fancy spending their first days as a married couple on the slopes. The small chalet sleeps two, giving you and your partner total privacy in a beautiful snowy setting. Enjoy breakfast – delivered daily to your door – on the chalet balcony, or relax in your hot tub with a glass of champagne (bottles of bubbles are also delivered daily in time for your return from skiing). Honeymoon upgrades are available for an additional £100. From £120 a night (chaletperdu.co.uk/the-mazot).

Hacienda de San Rafael, Seville, Spain

Striking the perfect balance between rustic and modern, this former olive-oil estate boasts magnificent infinity pools, a top Mediterranean restaurant and tranquil gardens. Massages and yoga classes can be arranged on request, or for something a bit more unusual, try your hand at flamenco dancing or enjoy hot-air-balloon rides across Arcos de la Frontera. With incredible food and breathtaking views, this stately home promises an unforgettable Spanish experience. From £194 a night (haciendadesanrafaelcom).

15Glasgow, Scotland

One of Bazaar's most underrated European city destinations, Glasgow is the perfect staycation for a cultural couple. Boutique hotel 15Glasgow is located just five miutes from the city centre and West End, making it within walking distance of many of Glasgow's top museums, galleries and theatres. From £99 a night (15glasgow.com).

Crillon le Brave, Provence, France

This honey-coloured hotel in Provence is surrounded by vineyards and olive groves, guaranteeing guests stunning views and an authentic taste of the region's many simple pleasures. Spread out across a village (the hotel developers bought up the old 17th- and 18th-century houses from locals, many of which had fallen into disrepair), Crillon le Brave is a hilltop haven. From £240 a night (crillonlebrave.com).

Riad Dar Maya, Essaouira, Morocco

This Moroccan hideaway is located in the laid-back beach town of Essaouira on the Atlantic coast, with ocean views and its own rooftop hot tub. Run by a British family, the hotel is close to the Medina. We recommend making use of the hotel's traditional marble hammam, followed by an in-room beauty treatment or massage. From £85 a night (riaddarmaya.com).

The Old Parsonage, Oxford, England

This 17th-century hotel is in the heart one of England's most historic cities. Guests are just a few minutes' walk from notable sites such as the Sheldonian Theatre and Christ Church Cathedral. To head further afield, hop on one of the hotel bicycles and explore the city the traditional Oxford way – but don't forget to visit the hotel's private library to be as studious as your local counterparts. From around £200 a night (oldparsonage-hotel.co.uk).

V Boutique Hotel, Cadiz, Spain

This restored 17th-century manor house located in Andalusia is an intimate 12-bedroom spa hotel. Choose from endless activities, including horseback riding across the beaches on your doorstep and whale-watching excursions. You'll also be able to glimpse Africa from the hotel's roof terrace. From £155 a night (hotelv-vejer.com).

Castile Hotel, Paris, France

Classic French elegance awaits at this hotel on Rue Cambon, next-door to the apartment of Coco Chanel and between three of the most famous squares in Paris: Concorde, Vendôme and Madeleine. It is also close to the Jardin des Tuileries and Opéra district. Having all these top spots on your doorstep means you and your partner can easily make the most of this romantic city over a long weekend. From around £215 per night (castile.com).

Trevose House, Cornwall, England

Make the most of the British seaside at Trevose House, situated on the shores of St Ives Bay. Whether you fancy a champagne cream tea for two on the beach, or would prefer a relaxing massage in the privacy of your own room, the friendly and welcoming staff at this beautiful guesthouse will be more than happy to help. From £180 a night (trevosehouse.co.uk).

The Sani Asterias Suites, Halkidiki, Greece

Each of the spacious suites at this hotel on mainland Greece has a private terrace and views overlooking either the Aegean Sea or the private marina. Spend your time lazing on the hotel's beach, playing tennis or enjoying the spa and fitness centre by day, and relaxing to the sound of live jazz sipping aperitifs or eating food from Michelin-starred chefs by night. From £175 a night (saniresort.gr).

Villa Cora, Florence, Italy

Widely considered to be one of the most beautiful hotels in the area, Villa Cora was originally built by aristocrat Baron Oppenheim for his wife in the 1870s. As a result the decor is appropriately opulent, with a nod to bourgeois fashions of the late 19th century. The hotel is located inside a centuries-old park that overlooks the Boboli Gardens, and boasts elegant grounds of its own. From £320 a night (villacora.it).

La Chèvre D'Or, French Riviera

Set in the mediaeval hilltop village of Eze, situated between Nice and Monaco, La Chèvre D'Or is the ultimate destination for couples wanting total secluded luxury. The hotel has four restaurants – including one that holds two Michelin stars – and a bar with a vast selection of the finest champagnes, cognacs and cigars. A must for panoramic views, incredible sunsets and internationally renowned cuisine. From around £150 a night (chevredor.com/uk).

JK Place, Capri, Italy

Steal a few days away in this sunny cliffside sanctuary on Capri. If you want to get active, you can: scuba diving, fishing and visits to the Blue Grotto or Augustus Gardens are all on offer. Or simply stay in the spa, emerging only to enjoy views over the Bay of Naples and Vesuvius in the distance as you dine on exquisite Mediterranean cuisine. From around £350 a night (jkcapri.com).
