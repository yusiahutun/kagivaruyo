Tony Berlin Biography, Net Worth, Married, Wife, Children, Facts

Tony Berlin is a media sensation, an American television reporter known as the host of the popular television show Good Morning America. He is also known for his work with some of the major media networks such as CNN, CBS, NBC, and ABC. He is currently President and CEO of Berlin Media Relation.

Here you can learn all about his biography, his family, his assets, and some interesting facts about one of America’s best TV shows.

Tony Berlin – Biography

The television reporter, who is an American by nationality and a white American by ethnicity, was born in Los Angeles, California, in 1968. He has a sister, and both were raised by their mother, who divorced their father when they were children.

The young Berliner was enrolled in a local school in his hometown, which he subsequently attended. Tony studied reportage at the Poynter Institute in 2002, from where he went on to study political science at Occidental College, as he had an interest in reporting. There he earned a BA degree in Political Science before completing an internship at the PBS News Hour during his Master’s studies at the Graduate School of Communication at the American University.

Professional Career

Tony Berlin’s professional career began when he joined the ABC morning show: Good Morning America. From his first appearance on the show, he won the hearts of millions of Americans and became quite famous within a short time. His work at ABC was followed in 1995 by his role as a reporter for WHIO-TV, where he worked for three years before joining WCCO-TV.

At WCCO-TV Twin Cities, he worked as a senior reporter for seven productive years before moving to WCBS-TV Channel 2 in New York where he worked as a local reporter.

After working in the industry for over a decade and having a wealth of transferable experience in journalism, he was appointed Head of Media Relations, followed by a promotion to Director of Media Relations and later President of the Media House.

Tony Berlin knows his onions when it comes to journalism in its entirety. Throughout his career, he has worked on many shows and stations including PBS News Hour, ABC’s Today Show, Good Morning America, CNBC, FOX News Channel, and Fox Business Network. In the print media, he has made a name for himself on stations such as The Huffington Post, Time Magazine, Wall Street Journal, The New York Times, USA Today, and Newsweek.

Family: Married, Wife, Children
