Why dont they tell Jocelyn about the photo?

Television shows have long utilized a potent narrative device: the conspiracy to withhold significant information from a central character. This trope, when employed skillfully, can infuse a plotline with suspense, create rich dramatic tension, or prompt moments of lighthearted humor, all while serving as a catalyst for character development and plot progression. A prime example of this trope in action is found in the television drama "The Idol", particularly in the narrative arc involving the main character, Jocelyn, her crew, and a potentially career-shattering photograph.

In "The Idol", Jocelyn, an emerging pop star, unknowingly becomes the subject of a leaked embarrassing photo. The critical aspect of this narrative is her crew's calculated decision to keep her oblivious to this predicament. Their deception is arguably motivated by an attempt to protect her, given Jocelyn's history of emotional instability and their fear that the revelation could disrupt her performances. The clandestine approach taken by her team, meant to preserve her focus, helps create an engaging narrative arc filled with suspense, leaving the audience on tenterhooks about when and how Jocelyn will discover the truth.

Jocelyn's crew handles the scandal by spinning a feminist narrative around it, thus transforming an embarrassing incident into an empowering statement. This twist not only mitigates the crisis but also consolidates Jocelyn's image as a sex-positive icon. It highlights the trope's potential for engendering compelling drama and offers insights into the high-stakes world of image management in the entertainment industry.

The trope also serves up comedy, as exemplified by Chaim's farcical solution to Jocelyn's discomfort during a photoshoot, wherein he locks the overbearing intimacy coordinator in a bathroom. This ludicrous action, intended to maintain Jocelyn's comfort, provides much-needed comic relief in an otherwise tension-filled narrative.

While "The Idol" showcases the conspiracy trope's capacity for drama and comedy, it's hardly the only show to use this device. The ever-popular sitcom "Friends" frequently employs this trope to create comedic situations. In "The One Where Everybody Finds Out", the characters engage in a comedic conspiracy where they withhold information about Chandler and Monica's relationship from each other, leading to a series of amusing misunderstandings. Similarly, in "The One With Five Steaks and an Eggplant", the group hides their financial disparity, resulting in a hilariously awkward situation when they receive an expensive restaurant bill.
